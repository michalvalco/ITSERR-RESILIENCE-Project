# Chapter Selection: Annotationes in Locos communes (1561)

**Author:** Leonard Stöckel
**Work:** *Annotationes in Philippi Melanchthonis Locos Praecipuos Theologicos* (1561)
**Purpose:** Select 2-3 chapters with high citation density for GNORM adaptation pilot study
**Date:** January 25, 2026

---

## Selection Criteria

For the pilot study to effectively test GNORM's transferability from medieval canon law to Reformation-era theological texts, selected chapters must:

1. **High Citation Density** — Abundant references to authorities (patristic, biblical, classical, contemporary)
2. **Diverse Citation Types** — Mix of biblical proof-texts, patristic quotations, and Reformation-era sources
3. **Structural Complexity** — Marginal annotations, nested references, abbreviated citations
4. **Manageable Length** — Sufficient material for 100+ annotations without overwhelming scope
5. **Scholarly Interest** — Doctrinally significant topics that demonstrate project relevance

---

## Background: Structure of the *Annotationes*

Stöckel's *Annotationes* is a commentary on Philip Melanchthon's *Loci Communes Theologici* (1543 edition), the foundational systematic theology of the Lutheran Reformation. The work follows Melanchthon's topical structure (*loci* method), providing annotations, expansions, and additional source references for each theological topic.

**Expected Citation Patterns:**

| Citation Type | Frequency | Example Formats |
|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Biblical | Very High | "Rom. 3:23", "Gen. 3", "Ioan. 1:14" |
| Augustinian | High | "Aug. de civ. Dei lib. 14", "Aug. in Psal. 31" |
| Other Patristic | Medium | "Chrys. in Matt. hom. 12", "Hieron. ad Gal." |
| Melanchthon | High | "Phil. in loc. de iust.", cross-references |
| Luther | Medium | "Luth. in Gal.", "Luth. de lib. arb." |
| Classical | Low-Medium | "Cic. de off.", "Arist. Eth." |

## Selected Chapters

### Primary Selection: 3 Chapters

#### 1. *De Peccato Originis* (On Original Sin)

**Rationale:**
- **Citation Density: VERY HIGH**
- Core Augustinian doctrine — extensive citations to Augustine's anti-Pelagian works
- Heavy biblical engagement (Genesis 3, Romans 5, Psalm 51)
- Controversial topic requiring authoritative support
- Melanchthon's treatment is detailed, giving Stöckel ample material to annotate

**Expected Citation Types:**
- Augustine: *De civitate Dei*, *Contra Iulianum*, *De natura et gratia*, *Enchiridion*
- Biblical: Genesis 3, Psalm 51, Romans 3, 5, 7
- Contemporary: Luther (*De servo arbitrio*), Melanchthon

**Estimated Annotations:** 40-60 references

---

#### 2. *De Iustificatione* (On Justification)

**Rationale:**
- **Citation Density: VERY HIGH**
- Central Reformation doctrine — the *articulus stantis et cadentis ecclesiae*
- Extensive scriptural proof-texting (Romans, Galatians especially)
- Engagement with patristic sources to demonstrate catholic continuity
- Polemical context requires marshaling authorities against Catholic opponents

**Expected Citation Types:**
- Biblical: Romans 1-8, Galatians 2-3, Ephesians 2, Philippians 3
- Patristic: Augustine (*De spiritu et littera*), Ambrose, Bernard of Clairvaux
- Luther: Commentary on Galatians, *De servo arbitrio*
- Contemporary: Melanchthon's *Apology of the Augsburg Confession*

**Estimated Annotations:** 50-70 references

---

#### 3. *De Lege et Evangelio* (On Law and Gospel)

**Rationale:**
- **Citation Density: HIGH**
- Foundational Lutheran hermeneutical distinction
- Requires extensive biblical proof from both Testaments
- Engages patristic sources on interpretation of Mosaic law
- Connects to practical preaching — may include homiletical examples

**Expected Citation Types:**
- Biblical: Exodus 20, Deuteronomy, Matthew 5-7, Romans 7, Galatians 3, 2 Corinthians 3
- Patristic: Augustine (law/grace distinction), Jerome (biblical interpretation)
- Luther: *Antinomian disputations*, preface to Romans
- Contemporary: Melanchthon on *usus legis*

**Estimated Annotations:** 35-50 references

## Comparison with GNORM Baseline

| Metric | GNORM (*Liber Extra*) | Stöckel Pilot (projected) |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Corpus size | 186 documents | 3 chapters |
| Total annotations (expert) | 18,425 tokens | ~150 references (target: 100+) |
| Primary reference type | Legal (canon law) | Theological (biblical, patristic) |
| Citation format | Abbreviated legal | Abbreviated scholarly |
| Language | Medieval Latin | Humanist Latin (16th c.) |

**Key Differences for Pipeline Adaptation:**
1. Biblical citation formats differ from legal citation formats
2. Author abbreviations follow different conventions (Aug. vs. X for Decretales)
3. Chapter:verse structure for Scripture vs. title:chapter for canon law
4. Mixed Latin-German passages possible in Stöckel
