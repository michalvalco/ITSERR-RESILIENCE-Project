# Fellowship Information

Details about the ITSERR Transnational Access Fellowship supporting this project.

## Fellowship Overview

**Program:** ITSERR Transnational Access (TNA) Fellowship

**Title:** *Designing Ethically-Grounded AI Agents for Religious Studies Research: From Personalist Anthropology to Technical Implementation*

**Dates:** February 10-27, 2026

**Host Institution:** University of Palermo (UniPa), Italy

**Host Team:** Software Lab, GNORM Project

## Fellow

**Prof. Michal Valco**

Comenius University Bratislava
Evangelical Lutheran Theological Faculty

### Background

- 20+ years in theological hermeneutics
- Research focus: intersection of theology and technology
- Interest: AI ethics from personalist perspective

### Project Motivation

> "How can AI tools support theological research while respecting the irreducible personhood of the researcher and the normative nature of theological claims?"

## Host Institution

### University of Palermo

Founded in 1806, UniPa is a major Italian research university with strengths in:

- Digital humanities
- Computer science
- Religious studies

### Software Lab

The Software Lab specializes in:

- Natural language processing
- Machine learning applications
- Digital research tools

### GNORM Project

**Generic NORmalization of Medieval texts**

- CRF-based named entity recognition
- Focus on religious and historical texts
- Part of ITSERR WP3 (T-ReS)

**Team Lead:** Dr. Arianna Maria Pavone

## Fellowship Objectives

### Theoretical

1. Articulate philosophical foundations for AI in religious studies
2. Develop epistemic modesty framework
3. Connect personalist anthropology to technical design

### Technical

1. Implement narrative memory system
2. Create epistemic indicator classification
3. Integrate with GNORM annotation services
4. Develop human-centered tool patterns

### Collaborative

1. Learn from GNORM team's technical expertise
2. Contribute ethical/philosophical perspective
3. Establish ongoing collaboration
